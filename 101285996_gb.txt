Content ABOUT George Brown College

George Brown College started its campuses in 1968, having just over 2,000 students. 
In addition to programs in construction, early childhood education, dental services and the culinary arts — subjects it continued to teach — it once offered programs that might not be quite as relevant today, like signwriting and watchmaking.
Believe it or not, it even prepared students for careers at sea through a marine engineering program.

Since then, George brown has full-time student population has grown to more than 30,000.
People come to George Brown from across the Greater Toronto Area, the country and the world to develop the skills and gain the real-world experience needed to succeed in the workplace.

George Brown adds a sprawling new building at 160 Kendal Ave.
The newly renamed Casa Loma Campus offers programs in construction, computer systems and networks, jewellery making and dance.
The campus was originally intended to provide programs with a technology and applied arts focus.

Five hospital nursing schools were merged under the George Brown College umbrella: Toronto General Hospital, St. Michael’s Hospital, St. Joseph’s Hospital, the Atkinson School of Nursing (at Toronto Western Hospital), and Nightingale School of Nursing.
